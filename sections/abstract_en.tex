\begin{sloppypar}
This thesis focuses on the design and implementation of an MRI medical image generation platform based on StyleGAN2 and 3D Gaussian Splatting. To address the challenges of limited medical imaging data, strict privacy constraints, insufficiently intuitive teaching demonstrations, and the weak spatial representation of two-dimensional generated slices, an integrated platform is developed to support data preprocessing, two-dimensional slice generation, continuous sequence construction, point cloud initialization, 3DGS training and reconstruction, and interactive visualization. Based on the IXI brain MRI dataset, the system completes NIfTI slicing, intensity normalization, resizing, quality filtering, and dataset construction. A StyleGAN2-ADA model is trained for two-dimensional MRI slice generation. For three-dimensional reconstruction, the system constructs 180 continuous MRI slices and a 300000-point initial point cloud, completes 30000 iterations of 3DGS training, and obtains a three-dimensional representation containing 572073 Gaussian points. The platform adopts Gradio as the interaction framework and supports preprocessing, model training, slice generation, 2D/3D result presentation, and AI-assisted question answering. Experimental results show that the StyleGAN2 model can generate two-dimensional slices with MRI-like grayscale structures. At iteration 30000, the 3DGS training achieves an L1 loss of 0.0709 and a PSNR of 17.5894 dB, while the mean global SSIM over 180 paired rendered and reference slices reaches 0.8935. The implemented platform provides a reproducible engineering foundation for medical imaging teaching, generative model experiments, and three-dimensional reconstruction research.
\end{sloppypar}
