This thesis focuses on the design and implementation of an MRI medical image generation platform based on StyleGAN2 and 3D Gaussian Splatting. To address the challenges of limited medical imaging data, strict privacy constraints, and insufficiently intuitive teaching demonstrations, an integrated platform is developed to support data preprocessing, two-dimensional slice generation, continuous sequence construction, three-dimensional point cloud initialization, and interactive visualization. Based on the IXI brain MRI dataset, the system completes NIfTI data slicing, normalization, resizing, and dataset construction. A StyleGAN2-based model is trained for two-dimensional MRI slice generation, and a subsequent pipeline is implemented for continuous slice sequence generation and initial point-cloud construction toward three-dimensional reconstruction. The platform adopts Gradio as the interaction framework and supports preprocessing, model training, slice generation, 2D/3D result presentation, and AI-assisted question answering. Experimental results show that the platform can complete the core workflow of MRI image generation and presentation, providing a practical engineering foundation for medical imaging teaching and intelligent generation research.
