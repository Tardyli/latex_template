\begin{sloppypar}
This thesis focuses on the design and implementation of an MRI medical image generation platform based on StyleGAN2 and 3D Gaussian Splatting. To address the challenges of limited medical imaging data, strict privacy constraints, insufficiently intuitive teaching demonstrations, and the weak spatial representation of two-dimensional generated slices, an integrated platform is developed to support data preprocessing, two-dimensional slice generation, continuous sequence construction, three-dimensional point cloud initialization, 3DGS-oriented interface adaptation, and interactive visualization. Based on the IXI brain MRI dataset, the system completes NIfTI slicing, intensity normalization, resizing, quality filtering, and dataset construction. A StyleGAN2-ADA model is trained for two-dimensional MRI slice generation. For three-dimensional representation, the system generates continuous slice sequences through latent-space interpolation, maps non-background pixels into spatial points, exports an initial PLY point cloud, and designs the input adaptation, parameter optimization, and result replacement workflow for subsequent 3DGS training. The platform adopts Gradio as the interaction framework and supports preprocessing, model training, slice generation, 2D/3D result presentation, and AI-assisted question answering. Experimental results show that the platform can complete the main workflow of MRI image generation and staged three-dimensional representation. The StyleGAN2 checkpoint, generated slices, and initial point cloud are traceable project artifacts, providing a practical engineering foundation for medical imaging teaching, generative model experiments, and future 3DGS-based reconstruction.
\end{sloppypar}
