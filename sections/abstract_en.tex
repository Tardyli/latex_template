\begin{sloppypar}
This thesis focuses on the design and implementation of an MRI medical image generation platform based on StyleGAN2 and 3D Gaussian Splatting. To address the challenges of limited medical imaging data, strict privacy constraints, insufficiently intuitive teaching demonstrations, and the weak spatial representation of two-dimensional generated slices, an integrated platform is developed to support data preprocessing, two-dimensional slice generation, continuous sequence construction, three-dimensional point cloud initialization, 3DGS input adaptation, and interactive visualization. Based on the IXI brain MRI dataset, the system completes NIfTI slicing, intensity normalization, resizing, quality filtering, and dataset construction. A StyleGAN2-ADA model is trained for two-dimensional MRI slice generation. For three-dimensional representation, the system generates continuous slice sequences through latent-space interpolation, maps non-background pixels into spatial points, exports a PLY point cloud, and builds a 3DGS workflow for spatial organization, parameter modeling, and platform presentation. The platform adopts Gradio as the interaction framework and supports preprocessing, model training, slice generation, 2D/3D result presentation, and AI-assisted question answering. Experimental results show that the platform can complete the main workflow of MRI image generation and three-dimensional representation. The StyleGAN2 checkpoint, generated slices, and point cloud are traceable project artifacts, providing a practical engineering foundation for medical imaging teaching, generative model experiments, and 3DGS reconstruction research.
\end{sloppypar}
