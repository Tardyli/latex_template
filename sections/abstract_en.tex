\begin{sloppypar}
This thesis presents the design and implementation of an MRI medical image generation platform that combines StyleGAN2 with 3D Gaussian Splatting. Motivated by the high acquisition cost, privacy sensitivity, and limited teaching accessibility of real MRI data, as well as the weak spatial expression of isolated two-dimensional generated slices, the proposed system integrates NIfTI preprocessing, MRI slice synthesis, continuous sequence organization, point cloud initialization, 3DGS-based reconstruction, and interactive visualization into a unified workflow. Using the IXI brain MRI dataset as the experimental source, the system performs volume slicing, intensity normalization, resolution standardization, low-information slice filtering, and StyleGAN2-ADA dataset construction. A two-dimensional MRI slice generator is then trained and connected to the platform inference module. For the three-dimensional stage, 180 continuous MRI slices and a 300000-point initial point cloud are prepared for 30000 iterations of 3DGS training, producing a final representation with 572073 Gaussian points. The platform is implemented with Gradio and supports data preprocessing, model training, slice generation, 2D/3D result inspection, and AI-assisted question answering. Experimental results indicate that the trained StyleGAN2 model can generate MRI-like slices with recognizable brain grayscale structures. At iteration 30000, the 3DGS training reaches an L1 loss of 0.0709 and a PSNR of 17.5894 dB, and the mean global SSIM over 180 rendered-reference slice pairs is 0.8935. The resulting platform establishes a reproducible engineering pipeline from MRI data processing to two-dimensional generation and three-dimensional Gaussian representation, providing practical support for medical image generation education, generative model experiments, and 3D reconstruction validation.
\end{sloppypar}
