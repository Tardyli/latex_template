\begin{sloppypar}
This thesis presents an MRI medical image generation platform. The platform combines StyleGAN2 and 3D Gaussian Splatting. Real MRI data is costly to collect. It is also sensitive because it may involve privacy and ethical review. A single generated two-dimensional slice is not enough to show spatial structure. To address these issues, the system connects NIfTI preprocessing, MRI slice generation, continuous sequence construction, point cloud initialization, 3DGS training, and interactive visualization in one workflow. The system uses the IXI brain MRI dataset as the experimental source. It performs volume slicing, intensity normalization, resolution standardization, low-information slice filtering, and StyleGAN2-ADA dataset construction. It then trains a two-dimensional MRI slice generator and connects the trained model to the platform. For the three-dimensional stage, the system prepares 180 continuous MRI slices and a 300000-point initial point cloud. It completes 30000 iterations of 3DGS training and produces a representation with 572073 Gaussian points. The platform is implemented with Gradio. It supports data preprocessing, model training, slice generation, 2D/3D result inspection, and AI-assisted question answering. Experimental results show that the trained StyleGAN2 model can generate MRI-like slices with recognizable brain grayscale structures. At iteration 30000, the 3DGS training reaches an L1 loss of 0.0709 and a PSNR of 17.5894 dB. The mean global SSIM over 180 rendered-reference slice pairs is 0.8935. The platform builds a reproducible engineering pipeline from MRI data processing to two-dimensional generation and three-dimensional Gaussian representation. It provides practical support for medical image generation education, generative model experiments, and 3D reconstruction validation.
\end{sloppypar}
